Executive Summary

Project Name Jacobs Project Number Potential Impact Date
J822 Former UBH International Ltd Orrell Lane Burscough B27050BH High Risk 28/05/2021

This summary report describes the approach used to undertake a high-level review of the available data associated with the new development at Orrell Lane, Burscough to establish if additional modelling work is required prior to carrying out a full development impact assessment (DIA).

A total of 65 residential properties have been proposed for a new development in Burscough catchment. Foul and surface-water flows from the development are to flow by gravity into the existing 675mm combined sewer off Orrell Lane via manhole SD43128301. The receiving sewer is predicted to flood during the 1 in 5-year design storm. The topography of the area dictates that overland flooding is directed away from the development in a northerly direction. Additional flows from the development are anticipated to increase this model predicted flooding, but to a small degree as the development flows are much smaller than the flows currently flowing through the receiving sewer (9.67l/s of total additional flows versus 557l/s currently predicted for the 30-year design storm).

There is a cluster of flooding incidents at New Lane (Ormskirk) on the downstream flow conveyance path, comprising 3 Internal and 5 External flooding register locations, 1 external WIRS incident, 2 INS external incidents and 1 HFRR incident. These existing issues are considered likely to be negatively impacted by the additional flows.

There are no cellared properties, tanks, or CSOs along the flow conveyance path. There is an existing pumping station on New Lane, however, the performance of this is unlikely to be impacted by the additional foul flows as this is not on the main flow conveyance path.

Spills to and from Burscough WwTW Storm Tanks could be impacted by the increase in flows from the development.

With reference to the above conclusions, the new development is considered a High Risk.

Project Objective

The aim of the project is to undertake a high-level review using the latest data currently available to make an informed decision as to the potential impact of the proposed new development on the existing sewer network; to also establish if a full development impact assessment will be required, and if there is likely to be any impact on the network that would potentially cause significant deterioration.

Summary of Triage phase work to be undertaken:

  • Review all data available from UU Developer Services and Catchment teams. The review is to include, but not be limited to:
  • Proposed / Outline drainage layout plans.
  • Gathering reports on hydraulic or other applicable investigations (e.g. IJO’s), and IDAS reports.
  • Confirm that sufficient information is available and advise the client where further information is required.
  • Confirm the point(s) of connection and flow rates to be considered in the investigation.
  • Review model performance data (where available) and summarise the hydraulic performance in the area of development and the receiving catchment, where it may potentially be impacted by development flows - particularly at registered flooding locations and hydraulically linked ancillaries.
  • Report on any flooders in the catchment which have the potential to be influenced by the new development, consider model flooding and reported flood frequency.
  • Complete a high-level (triage) review of the model and establish if any model updates are required in order to progress to a full development impact assessment.
  • Identify where further analysis is not required.

Conclusion

Foul and surface-water flows from the development are to flow by gravity into the existing 675 mm combined sewer off Orrell Lane via manhole SD43128301. The receiving sewer is predicted to flood during the 1 in 5-year design storm and to cause overland flooding of the farmland to the north of the development site. Additional flows from the development are expected to worsen this flooding, but to a small degree because these additional flows are much smaller than the flows currently flowing through the receiving sewer (9.67l/s of total additional flows versus 557l/s currently predicted for the 30-year design storm).

These existing issues are considered likely to be negatively impacted by the additional flows.
